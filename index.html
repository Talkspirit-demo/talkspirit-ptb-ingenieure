Your context

Your challenges, our answer

What we understood
from your situation

"We manage ~50 meetings a week for DB Netz. The challenge: moving from coordination by email and Teams to a system where every issue becomes a clear, assigned, and tracked action."

~50 meetings/week with no shared structure

The project controls team manages dozens of meetings for DB Netz — with no shared agenda, no decision tracking, no link between discussions and the actions that need to follow.

Structured meeting module: floating agenda, check-in, live action assignment — every issue raised becomes a task with an owner.

14 project controllers, no shared workspace

Information comes via GRR, email and Teams — tools imposed by the client. There is no common internal space for the team, as a critical construction phase approaches.

Dedicated project groups with chat, drive and newsfeed — one space for the 14-person team, accessible from site or office.

Issues raised, but never tracked

When someone raises a problem in a meeting, there is no direct path to an assigned, tracked action. Items fall through the cracks between weekly check-ins.

Project Kanban boards connected to meetings — an issue raised in a meeting instantly becomes a task with a date, owner, and status.
